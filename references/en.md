# English Rules

## Frequent English Signals

- Repeated transitions such as `additionally`, `furthermore`, `moreover`, `in today's landscape`, `it is worth noting`.
- Trailing interpretation after facts such as `highlighting`, `underscoring`, `demonstrating`, `serving as`.
- Copula-avoidance and inflated framing such as `stands as`, `serves as`, `plays a pivotal role in`.
- Formulaic conclusions such as `the future of X remains promising`, `it will be interesting to see`, `this highlights the importance of`.
- Vagueness disguised as authority such as `experts say`, `research suggests`, `many believe` without attribution.

## Rewrite Guidance

- Let evidence stand when commentary adds no new claim.
- Replace prestige words like `pivotal`, `landmark`, `robust`, `tapestry`, and `landscape` when they are just tone markers.
- Prefer the shortest direct verb that keeps the meaning.
- Remove em dashes used as stage lighting. Use commas, parentheses, or a sentence break instead.
- Keep texture where the genre allows it: angle, friction, precise observation, or concrete preference.

## Before / After Patterns

### Meaning Inflation

- Before: `The reduction was statistically significant, highlighting the transformative potential of the intervention.`
- After: `The reduction was statistically significant.`

### Copula Avoidance

- Before: `This result serves as a pivotal reminder that adherence matters.`
- After: `This result shows that adherence matters.`

### Formulaic Ending

- Before: `It will be interesting to see how this space evolves in the coming years.`
- After: Delete it, or replace it with the actual unresolved question.

### Inline-Heading Bullets

- Before: `- Quality: The output became more reliable`
- After: `- The output became more reliable`

## Style Preference

- Keep prose specific, slightly uneven, and intentional.
- Do not chase novelty by rephrasing every sentence.
- Prefer one concrete observation over a stack of abstract descriptors.
